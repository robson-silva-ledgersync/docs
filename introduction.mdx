LedgerSync connects to your end-users' bank accounts and gives you back their
**accounts, balances, transactions, and statements** through one clean,
source-agnostic REST API. Under the hood we route across multiple data sources;
you only ever touch one interface.

## The mental model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Client">
    A **Client** is your record of one end-user, the person whose bank you'll read.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a Connection">
    A **Connection** links one bank to that client. You start it, the user logs
    in, we handle the aggregation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read their data">
    Once active, pull **accounts**, **transactions**, and **statements**, and
    receive **webhooks** as things change.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Everything is JSON over HTTPS, and sandbox is one key away. Bank credentials
  are entered only on LedgerSync-hosted pages, never through your servers.
</Note>
